Comparison of online music lockers

This is a comparison of online music storage services, Internet services that allow uploads of personally owned or licensed music to the cloud for listening on multiple devices.<ref>...</ref>

There are currently three large services,<ref>...</ref> each incorporating an online music store (see comparison), with purchased songs from the associated music store not counting toward storage limits. Both Amazon and Apple provided a small amount of storage space at no cost with additional storage for a price. Other than additional storage space, the main additional feature provided with an annual fee by Amazon and Apple is "scan-and-match", which examines music files on a computer and adds a copy of matched tracks the user's music locker without having to upload the files. Google provides both a large amount of storage space and the scan-and-match feature at no cost.

While Apple negotiated with the major record labels for a license before launching their service, Amazon and then Google launched their services without obtaining any license from the labels.<ref>...</ref> Amazon and Google eventually negotiated licenses before launching their scan-and-match features.

Unlike other services, Amazon does not allow podcasts, ringtones, or audiobooks to be uploaded.<ref ...>...</ref><ref>...</ref>

For streaming services where a person is unable to upload their own music, but is limited to music provided by the service, such as Pandora and Spotify, see On-demand streaming music services.

Former or defunct services

  • My.MP3.com started in January 2000, fought major record labels in UMG v. MP3.com, and the service was discontinued by a new owner.<ref>...</ref>
  • MP3tunes started in late 2005, fought major record labels in Capitol Records, Inc. v. MP3Tunes, LLC, and closed in 2012 after filing for Chapter 7 bankruptcy.<ref>...</ref>
  • Lala started in 2006, was purchased by Apple, and shutdown on May 31, 2010.
  • mSpot Music started in May 2010,<ref>...</ref> was purchased by Samsung,<ref>...</ref> and shutdown on October 15, 2012.
  • Best Buy Music Cloud debuted in June 2011 to horrible reviews.<ref>...</ref>
  • Mashup started in 2010 as Mougg,<ref>...</ref><ref>...</ref> and the domain was no longer active as of December 2012.
